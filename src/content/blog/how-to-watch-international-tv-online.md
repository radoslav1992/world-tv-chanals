Twenty years ago, watching television from another country meant a satellite dish pointed at the right part of the sky and an expensive subscription. Today, all you need is a web browser. This guide explains how to watch international TV channels online — for free — and how to get the best experience.

## Everything happens in your browser

[World TV Channels](/) plays live streams directly in your browser using standard web video technology. There's nothing to install, no account to create and no set-top box to buy. Open the site, pick a channel, and live television starts playing.

Under the hood, channels are delivered using **HLS** — the same streaming standard used by most live video on the web — and played with an open-source video engine. On Apple devices the browser handles this natively; on others a small library does the work automatically.

## Finding the channel you want

With thousands of channels available, good navigation matters:

- **Search** by name to jump straight to a channel you know.
- **Filter by category** — news, sport, movies, music, kids and more.
- **Filter by country** to explore what's on air in a particular part of the world.
- **Browse page by page**, so the list stays fast even with thousands of channels.

When you find channels you love, tap the **star** to save them to your favorites, and check your **history** to return to something you watched earlier. These are stored privately in your browser — nothing is sent to a server.

## Getting the best picture

Live streaming quality depends mostly on your internet connection. A few tips:

- Use **Wi-Fi or a wired connection** for the smoothest playback.
- If a stream stutters, give it a few seconds — adaptive streaming will usually settle on a quality your connection can handle.
- Use the **fullscreen** button for a proper TV experience.

## When a channel won't play

Live streams are maintained by broadcasters and the open-source community, so they occasionally change or go offline. If a channel won't load, it may be temporarily unavailable, may have moved, or may require settings a browser can't supply. The simplest solution is to try another channel — there are always thousands more.

## Where the channels come from

The listings are powered by the community-maintained [iptv-org](https://github.com/iptv-org/iptv) project, an open collection of publicly available streams from around the world. The actual broadcasts belong to the respective channels; World TV Channels simply helps you find and play the publicly available streams in one convenient place.

## The world, one click away

The beauty of online TV is reach. In a single session you can watch the morning news in London, a documentary in Berlin, a talk show in Madrid and breaking news from across the Atlantic. [Start exploring](/) and see just how much of the world's television is now a click away.
