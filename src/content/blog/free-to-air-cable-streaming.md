Television has never been easier to watch — or more confusing to navigate. Between aerials, satellite dishes, cable boxes and a growing pile of streaming apps, it's worth stepping back to understand the main ways we receive TV today and what each one is good for.

## Free-to-air

Free-to-air television is broadcast over the airwaves and picked up with an antenna. In much of Europe and North America it has gone digital — platforms such as Freeview in the UK and digital terrestrial television elsewhere — offering dozens of channels at no monthly cost. Public broadcasters like the BBC, PBS, Rai and RTVE are typically available this way.

**Strengths:** free, reliable, no subscription. **Limits:** fewer channels than pay-TV, and reception depends on your location.

## Cable and satellite

Cable and satellite providers bundle large numbers of channels — including premium sport, film and entertainment networks — for a monthly fee. Satellite reaches rural areas that cable can't, while cable often bundles internet and phone service.

**Strengths:** the widest selection of channels, premium content. **Limits:** cost, contracts and equipment.

## Streaming

Streaming delivers television over the internet. It comes in several flavours: on-demand services with their own original shows, live-TV services that replace a traditional cable package, and free ad-supported channels. Because streaming travels over the open internet, it also makes it possible to watch channels from other countries — something traditional broadcasting never allowed.

**Strengths:** watch anywhere, on any device; access to international channels; often free or low-cost. **Limits:** needs a decent internet connection, and live streams can occasionally drop.

## The bottom line

There is no single "best" way to watch TV — it depends on what you want. Free-to-air is perfect for the essentials, cable and satellite for the widest premium selection, and streaming for flexibility and international reach. The happy reality of 2026 is that most of us mix and match, choosing whichever route gets us to the programme we want.
