## Overview

CNN (Cable News Network) is an American news channel and one of the most recognised news brands in the world. Founded by media entrepreneur Ted Turner, it launched on 1 June 1980 as the first television channel to provide 24-hour news coverage, transforming the way audiences consume news.

The network operates a domestic US channel alongside CNN International, which reaches viewers in nearly every country. CNN is owned by Warner Bros. Discovery.

## History

When CNN launched in 1980, the idea of a round-the-clock news channel was untested. The network proved its value during major breaking stories, including its landmark live coverage of the 1991 Gulf War, which established 24-hour news as an essential part of the media landscape.

CNN went on to build a global network of bureaux and launched a family of channels and digital products, adapting over the decades to satellite, cable, the web and, more recently, streaming services.

## Programming

CNN's schedule combines rolling breaking-news coverage with anchored programmes, political analysis, business reporting and documentary strands. The network is known for its election-night coverage, live reporting from the field, and town-hall and debate broadcasts that play a prominent role in American political life.

## Significance

As the original 24-hour news network, CNN had a profound influence on broadcast journalism worldwide and inspired a generation of competitors. Its international service remains a key source of US and global news for audiences around the world.
