## Overview

CBS (the Columbia Broadcasting System) is one of the major commercial broadcast television networks in the United States. It received its commercial television licence in 1941 and grew from the CBS radio network founded in the 1920s. The network is owned by Paramount Global and is identified by its iconic "eye" logo.

## History

CBS became known as the "Tiffany Network" for the perceived quality of its programming. Its news division set the standard for American broadcast journalism, particularly through pioneering correspondents in the mid-20th century. The network has remained a ratings leader across many eras of American television.

CBS content is now available on demand through the Paramount+ streaming service.

## Programming

CBS is recognised for popular scripted dramas and comedies, procedural franchises, and reality competition formats. Its news operation, CBS News, produces the long-running newsmagazine *60 Minutes* and flagship evening and morning bulletins. The network also carries major sports rights, including coverage of the NFL and college basketball.

## Significance

As one of America's "Big Three" legacy networks, CBS has been a defining presence in US broadcasting for decades, combining mass-audience entertainment with one of the most respected news organisations in the country.
