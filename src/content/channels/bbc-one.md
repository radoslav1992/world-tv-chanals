## Overview

BBC One is the flagship television channel of the British Broadcasting Corporation and one of the most widely watched channels in the United Kingdom. As a public-service broadcaster funded primarily by the television licence fee, BBC One carries no commercial advertising and offers a broad mix of drama, entertainment, factual programming, sport and news.

The channel traces its origins to the launch of the BBC Television Service on 2 November 1936, widely regarded as the start of the world's first regular high-definition television service. It was renamed BBC1 in 1964 when BBC2 launched, and adopted the "BBC One" styling in 1997.

## History

BBC Television began broadcasting from Alexandra Palace in London in 1936, suspended service during the Second World War, and resumed in 1946. Over the following decades it became the centrepiece of British broadcasting, introducing colour transmission in 1969 and a succession of iconic idents, including the long-running rotating globe.

The channel has continually evolved with technology, launching a high-definition simulcast and integrating with the BBC iPlayer streaming service, which allows viewers to watch live and on demand across devices.

## Programming

BBC One is known for landmark drama and continuing series, long-running soap *EastEnders*, natural-history landmarks produced with the BBC Studios Natural History Unit, entertainment formats such as *Strictly Come Dancing*, and comprehensive coverage of national events. Its news bulletins, produced by BBC News, are among the most watched in the country.

The channel also broadcasts regional variations across England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland, providing local news and programming tailored to each nation and region.

## Significance

As a public broadcaster, BBC One occupies a central place in British cultural life, providing universal access to information, education and entertainment. Its coverage of national moments — royal events, general elections and major sporting fixtures — regularly draws some of the largest audiences in the UK.
