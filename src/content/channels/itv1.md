## Overview

ITV1 is the flagship channel of ITV, the largest commercial broadcaster in the United Kingdom. It began broadcasting in 1955 as the country's first commercial television service, providing an advertising-funded alternative to the BBC. The channel has carried several names over the years and is operated by ITV plc.

## History

ITV launched as a network of regional commercial franchises, each serving a different part of the UK. Over the decades these franchises consolidated, and the main channel was unified under national branding. The channel adopted the "ITV1" name in the early 2000s and has continued to evolve, most recently centring its on-demand offering on the ITVX streaming platform.

## Programming

ITV1 is known for mass-audience entertainment, including long-running soaps such as *Coronation Street* and *Emmerdale*, popular reality and talent formats, primetime drama, and major live events. Its news is provided by ITV News, produced by ITN, offering an independent alternative to the BBC's bulletins.

## Significance

As the UK's leading commercial channel, ITV1 has been central to British popular culture since the 1950s. It introduced advertising-funded television to the country and remains a dominant force in entertainment and drama.
