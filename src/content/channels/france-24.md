## Overview

France 24 is France's international news channel, launched on 6 December 2006. Operated by the public group France Médias Monde, it broadcasts continuous news in several languages — including French, English, Arabic and Spanish — offering a French and European perspective on world affairs.

## History

France 24 was created to give France a global news voice comparable to international broadcasters such as the BBC and CNN. It expanded steadily, adding language services and growing its distribution across satellite, cable and digital platforms, with a strong presence on online and social video.

## Programming

The channel offers rolling news bulletins, in-depth debate and analysis programmes, business and culture coverage, and reporting from a network of international correspondents. Its multilingual model allows it to tailor coverage to audiences in different regions while maintaining a consistent editorial identity.

## Significance

France 24 is a key part of France's international media presence and a widely available source of global news. Its free online and YouTube streams have made it particularly accessible to audiences worldwide.
