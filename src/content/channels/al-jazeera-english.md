## Overview

Al Jazeera English is the English-language international news channel of the Qatar-based Al Jazeera Media Network. Launched on 15 November 2006, it was one of the first global English-language news channels headquartered outside the United States and Europe, and it places particular emphasis on coverage of the developing world.

## History

Al Jazeera English grew out of the influential Arabic-language Al Jazeera channel, which launched in 1996 and transformed news coverage in the Arab world. The English service was built around a network of broadcasting centres in Doha, London, Washington and Kuala Lumpur, allowing it to "follow the sun" and hand off live coverage around the globe.

## Programming

The channel offers rolling news, documentaries, current-affairs and investigative programming, and analysis from a network of correspondents across Africa, Asia, the Middle East and Latin America. It is known for giving prominence to stories and regions often underreported by other international broadcasters.

## Significance

Al Jazeera English brought a distinctive perspective to global news, broadening the range of voices in international broadcasting. Its reporting from the developing world and its award-winning documentaries have earned it a substantial international audience.
